Core system

Logo, mark, and color language

The product should read as RoboQC first. Romeo FlexVision appears as a supporting attribution, not as the main headline. Keep the interface dark, precise, and industrial with a strong blue accent.

Logo system

Use the full RoboQC lockup for headers and proposal screens. Use the mark or icon for favicons, touch icons, avatars, and compact UI placements.

Primary lockup RoboQC is the visible brand for landing pages, decks, and demos.
Supporting signature Use "powered by Romeo FlexVision" as a subtitle or footer-level attribution.

Palette

Keep contrast crisp. Blue does the signaling. The dark neutrals carry the operational tone.

Primary Blue #265CD1 for CTA buttons, highlights, badges, and active states.
Dark Gray #1F2937 for depth, industrial tone, and high-contrast framing.
Background gradient Use a deep dark gradient with restrained blue bloom rather than flat black.

Icon kit

The robot-camera icon is the quick recognition asset for browser chrome and compact UI.

Messaging anchors

Copy should sound alert, industrial, and immediate. Talk about stations, defects, traces, and proof.

Hero line RoboQC. Your robot-camera on every station.
Operational promise Catch 90% of defects in real time.
Positioning sentence The robot-camera that catches mistakes on station #2, not at test #5.

Usage notes

Keep the stack hierarchy stable so product positioning stays clear across demos and proposals.

Do Lead with RoboQC in headlines, buttons, and navigation.
Do Use Romeo FlexVision as a technology badge or footer-level attribution.
Avoid Letting the stack name overpower the product brand in top-level messaging.

Visual direction

Use the ROMEO factory image for hero, proposal covers, and operational demo contexts where the robotic system needs to feel deployed, vigilant, and physically present on the line.

Morning-ready handoff

Landing, icons, palette, supporting attribution, and the standalone brand page are now aligned around RoboQC. This page can be shared directly as a quick visual guide while the product continues to ship on the existing Romeo FlexVision infrastructure.
