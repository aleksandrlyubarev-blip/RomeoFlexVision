Romeo FlexVision Brand Guide

This guide aligns the site with company rules: white-first surfaces, primary blue accents, disciplined gray neutrals, and a clear hierarchy between the Romeo FlexVision master brand and the RoboQC product layer.

Brand rules

Logo, palette, and application hierarchy

Company-facing pages should stay light, structured, and blue-led. RoboQC remains the product layer for pilots and demos, while Romeo FlexVision defines the corporate design system.

Master brand and product layer

Use the Romeo FlexVision lockup for company headers, proposals, and partnership surfaces. Use RoboQC for product messaging, pilot landing pages, and CTA-driven demo flows.

Powered signature Use "powered by Romeo FlexVision" as a subtitle, technology badge, or footer attribution.

Palette

Use a clean white base, one authoritative blue, and a restrained gray scale for corporate surfaces.

Primary Blue #265CD1 for CTA buttons, highlights, badges, and active states.
Dark Gray #333333 for headings, separators, and high-contrast corporate framing.
Gray #6B7280 for supporting copy, secondary controls, and neutral dividers.
Light Gray #F0F1F5 for panel fills, secondary surfaces, and calm structure.
White #FFFFFF for primary page surfaces, contrast, and browser-level clarity.

Icon kit

The robot-camera icon is the quick recognition asset for browser chrome and compact UI.

Messaging anchors

Copy should sound alert, industrial, and immediate. Talk about stations, defects, traces, and proof.

Hero line RoboQC. Your robot-camera on every station.
Operational promise Catch 90% of defects in real time.
Positioning sentence The robot-camera that catches mistakes on station #2, not at test #5.

Usage notes

Keep the stack hierarchy stable so product positioning stays clear across demos and proposals.

Do Lead with RoboQC in headlines, buttons, and navigation.
Do Use Romeo FlexVision as a technology badge or footer-level attribution.
Avoid Letting the stack name overpower the product brand in top-level messaging.

Visual direction

Use the ROMEO factory image for hero, proposal covers, and operational demo contexts where the robotic system needs to feel deployed, vigilant, and physically present on the line.
